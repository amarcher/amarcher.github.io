    Pick one value

    Next pick one value explain why it's important to you.

    For example, let me give an account for why compassion is important to me. When I'm at my happiest it is because I feel a sense of inclusion and belonging, and I get this feeling when people -- my girlfriend, my family, my close friends -- show they understand and care for me and care about me: my opinion, my voice, my comfort. Their compassion towards me is essential to feel that inclusion. I also feel rewarded when I act compassionately and show an appreciation towards others even in small ways: like including someone in a conversation they've been left out of, or asking them how their day has been. I feel satisfied having provided that acknowledgement and inclusion to someone else.

    Advice

    Now think: what was the last topic that someone asked you for your advice on?

    For me, this was my girlfriend asking me for advice on how to respond to someone who had offended her in a text message. I think that I tried to help her show compassion and understanding in her response while communicating the disappointment she felt.

    How Did This Feel?

    Finally think: what has this exercise of thinking about your values made you feel? Would it help you to think about your values if you're feeling low?

    I know this exercise helped me form perspective on what I value in life. It's refreshing to take that long list of possible values and narrow it down to just the ones that I care about. I think narrowing it further would provide even more satisfaction because I wouldn't have to rate myself as underperforming on values that I hold in lower esteem. How did you feel?
